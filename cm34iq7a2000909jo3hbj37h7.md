### Walkthrough

#### The Workaround: Transfer, Don’t Archive

Instead of archiving the repositories you no longer need, you can transfer them to a new organization.

**Here’s a probably unnecessary step-by-step guide:**

1.  **Create a New Organization:**

*   Go to the ‘Organizations’ tab on GitHub.
*   Create a new organization.
*   Name it something indicative, like “your-organization-archive” or “graveyard”.

**2\. Transfer Your Repositories:**

*   Instead of archiving the repository, click on ‘Transfer’.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1710277482805/a032b08a-bbbf-4127-9570-a5c784432ff5.png)

*   Select the new organization as the destination.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1710277484243/75c027f1-95dd-44dc-8cce-fe6596cf9429.png)

### CAUTION:

If you’re on a PAID plan there are things that your repo will lose that you may want:

*   Code owners  
    \- Any existing wikis  
    \- Pulse, Contributors, Community, Traffic, Commits, Code Frequency, Network, and Forks on the — — Insights tab  
    \- Draft PRs  
    \- Multiple assignees for issues and PRs  
    \- Multiple reviewers for PRs  
    \- Branch and tag protection rules

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1710277486520/b8217176-9bec-480e-af36-1f7be81d3814.png)

But now these repositories won’t show up in your primary account’s search!

but they will remain accessible under the new organization.

### Unforeseen Benefits?

There’s a speculative bonus to this method.

Transferring repositories to a different organization might free up some storage space on your primary account.

It’s not confirmed, but it’s a potential additional benefit to this workaround.
